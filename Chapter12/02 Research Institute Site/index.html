Welcome to BabyGenes

We are a research group established in 1998 to seek out the causes and risk factors for various genetic defects in newborns. Currently, we investigate three kinds of factors -- parental genetics, parental behavior before and during pregnancy, and enviromental factors. Our goal is to help potential parents make good pre-natal decisions, to maximize their chances of having normal, healthy babies.

Before considering pregnancy, both parents should get a comprehensive genetic test done, especially if they have certain risk factors such as:

  • Narcolepsy, sleep apnea, or blackouts
  • Major depression or anxiety
  • Alcoholism or schizophrenia
  • Family history of genetic disorder
  • Amnesia or paralysis

Radiation and chemical carcinogens are the biggest environmental hazards to a developing baby's genes. Pregnant women should avoid smoking and, if possible, smoggy urban areas.

Unless you're bringing your baby to term on a spaceship or in a uranium mine, large does of radiation probably won't be an issue. But if you are concerned about it, you can get a home radon detector for about $100.

Research has shown that a parent's behavior before conception can have a significant effect on a baby's genetic health. People considering parenthood are strongly encouraged to avoid:

  • Handling radioactive solutions
  • Watching your food cook in the microwave
  • Excessive drug use
  • Smoking and other carcinogens

Remember, an unborn baby shares its mother's bloodstream. Anything that enters the mother's blood will affect the fetus and affect the development of its delicate organs.

Pregnant women should try to avoid industrial settings because of possible exposure to chemical fumes such as gasoline and paint solvents.
